# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Dystronic is for students, makers, robotics hobbyists, and electronics buyers who need a shorter path from idea to working prototype. They arrive needing parts, kits, learning help, or a guided AI plan for a concrete build.

## Product Purpose

The site presents Dystronic as a combined electronics store, learning lab, and AI project planner. Success means visitors immediately understand what can be built, see real components, and know how to request help or buy the parts.

## Brand Personality

Technical, energetic, maker-focused. The experience should feel like an active electronics lab: precise, visual, practical, and ready to help someone build today.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS landing pages, hidden product imagery, slow conversion paths, and form-heavy demos that make the user work before seeing value. Avoid designs where the product photos, WhatsApp contact, or AI Builder value are buried below the fold.

## Design Principles

- Show the build first: product images and the RC car demo must be visible before asking for effort.
- Convert in the first viewport: WhatsApp and AI Builder CTAs should be obvious and direct.
- Keep the lab credible: use real component imagery, concrete project language, and stock/parts details.
- Reduce form friction: request flows should prioritize WhatsApp and keep optional details secondary.
- Preserve the technical identity: dark lab surface, cyan/green accents, compact data-rich panels.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Keep body text readable against dark surfaces, avoid relying on color alone for state, support reduced-motion users, and ensure primary CTAs remain visible and usable on mobile.
